About

I am a junior undergraduate student pursuing Applied Mathematics at the University of California, Berkeley. My research interests lie at the intersection of large language models (LLMs) and multimodal AI systems, with a particular focus on LLM agents and vision-language models (VLLMs).

I am passionate about developing intelligent systems that can understand and interact with the world through multiple modalities. My recent work explores narrative coherence in AI-generated content, visual geolocation through multimodal models, and practical applications of LLMs in real-world scenarios.

    • GeoLocSFT: Efficient Visual Geolocation via Supervised Fine-Tuning of Multimodal Foundation Models
      Qiang Yi
      NeurIPS 2025 (Under Review)
      We introduce GeoLocSFT, a framework demonstrating how targeted supervised fine-tuning (SFT) of a large multimodal foundation model using only ~2700 carefully selected image-GPS pairs can yield highly competitive geolocation performance. Our approach achieves robust results on standard benchmarks while requiring significantly less training data compared to prior methods.

    • SCORE: Story Coherence and Retrieval Enhancement for AI Narratives
      Qiang Yi, Yangfan He, Jianhui Wang, Xinyuan Song, ShiYao Qian, Xinhang Yuan, Li Sun, Yi Xin, Jingqun Tang, Keqin Li, Kuan Lu, Jiaqi Chen, Tianyu Shi
      EMNLP 2025 System Demonstrations (Under Review) First Author
      We propose SCORE, a framework for detecting and resolving narrative inconsistencies in LLM-generated stories. By tracking key item statuses and generating episode summaries, SCORE uses RAG with TF-IDF and cosine similarity to identify related episodes and enhance story structure.
      [OpenReview] [Project Page] [Code]
    • KOLIntel: Making Crypto Influence Transparent, One Tweet at a Time
      Yanfu Zhang*, Hanlin Zhang*, Qiang Yi*, Zhimo Han, Yijin Wang, Tianze Xia, Xinyuan Song, Yangfan He, Yi Xin, Jianhui Wang, Tianyu Shi
      (*Equal contribution)
      EMNLP 2025 System Demonstrations (Under Review) Co-first Author
      KOLIntel is a modular framework for evaluating crypto Key Opinion Leaders and their promotional content on Twitter. The system combines LLM-based post analysis, engagement metric extraction, and bot/spam detection to generate multi-dimensional assessments of influencer credibility and content quality.
